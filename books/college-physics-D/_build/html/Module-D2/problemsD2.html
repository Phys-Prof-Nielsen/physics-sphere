Detailed Solutions#

Problem D2.1

Problem D2.1

Problem
Calculate the linear momentum of the following:

  1. A proton moving with in the +x-direction with a speed that is 99.9% the speed of light.

  2. A 2000.0 kg sedan traveling with a velocity of -70.0 mph.

Methodology
This is a plug and chuck approach by using

\( p_x = mv_x \)

to calculate the momentum of each object. We will need to look up the mass of a proton and the speed of light. Be careful to include enough significant figures. The mass of a proton is \(1.67262E-27\) kg and the speed of light is \(2.99792E8\) m/s. Also, we must remember to convert the velocity of the sedan into standard metric units. I use google for that purpose: 70.0 mph = 31.3 m/s.

Solution
Plugging in our numbers, we get

Proton:
\( p_x = (1.67262\times 10^{-27})(+2.99792\times 10^8)(0.999) = +5.01\times 10^{-19}~\textrm{kgm/s} \)

Sedan (answer to three significant figures):
\( p_x = (2000.0)(-31.3) = -6.26\times 10^4~\textrm{kgm/s} \)

Problem D2.2

Problem D2.2

Problem

A semi truck with a mass of 1.20E4 kg and speed of 65 mph is northbound on I-15. At the same time, a sedan with a mass of 2.40E3 kg is southbound on I-15 with a speed of 85 mph. What are the individual momentums and the magnitudes of momentums of the two vehicles? Consider the northbound direction to be positive.

Methodology
Similar to previous problem, this is a plug and chuck approach (remember to change units). The momentum is

\( p_x = mv_x \)

while the magnitude is

\( p = mv \)

Remember, \(v_x\) is the velocity while \(v\) is the speed.

Using google for unit conversions, we have 65 mph = 29 m/s and 85 mph = 38 m/s.

Solution
Pluggin our values:

Momentum of semi truck:

\( p_x = (1.20\times 10^4)(+29) = +3.5\times 10^5~\textrm{kgm/s} \)

Momentum of sedan:

\( p_x = (2.40\times 10^3)(-38) = -9.1\times 10^4~\textrm{kgm/s} \)

The magnitudes of the momentums are simply the momentums with the magnitude removed, or we can calculate them:

Magnitude of momentum of semi truck:

\( p_x = (1.20\times 10^4)(29) = 3.5\times 10^5~\textrm{kgm/s} \)

Magnitude of momentum of sedan:

\( p_x = (2.40\times 10^3)(38) = 9.1\times 10^4~\textrm{kgm/s} \)
