Problem D5.1

A 5.0 kg box is being pushed across a horizontal surface through a +235 N net force. The box is initially at rest at \(x = 0\) m. After being pushed a distance of 45 m,

  1. how much work was done on the box?

  2. what is the kinetic energy at 45 m?

  3. how fast is it traveling at 45 m?

  4. what is its velocity at 45 m?

Show code cell source Hide code cell source 
%reset -f

import sympy as sym

m = 5.0
fx = 235
dx = 45
v1 = 0.0
k1 = 0.0

# Part 1
# ------
w = sym.Symbol('w')
eq1 = w - fx*dx
sol1 = sym.solve(eq1, w)
w = sol1[0]
print('The work done on the box is: '+str(w)+' J.')

# Part 2
# ------
k2 = sym.Symbol('k2')
eq2 = (k2 - k1) - w
sol2 = sym.solve(eq2, k2)
k2 = sol2[0]
print('The kinetic energy at 45 m is: '+str(k2)+' J.')

# Part 3
# ------
v2 = sym.Symbol('v2')
eq3 = k2 - 0.5*m*(v2**2)
sol3 = sym.solve(eq3, v2)
v2 = sol3[1]
print('The speed at 45 m is: '+str(v2)+' m/s.')

# Part 3
# ------
# Since the object is moving in +x direction, the velocity is positive.
print('The speed at 45 m is: +'+str(v2)+' m/s.')

Problem D5.2

During a pumpkin drop, a 9.5 kg pumpkin is dropped from the roof of a 35 m building. The force exterted by gravity on the pumpkin is \( f_g = mg \), where \(g = 9.8~\textrm{m/s}^2\). Ignore air resistance. How fast is the pumpkin traveling upon impact with the ground assuming it is being thrown downwards with a speed of 25 m/s?

Show code cell source Hide code cell source 
%reset -f
import sympy as sym

m = 9.5
h = 35
g = 9.8
v1 = 25

# work
fx = m*g
dx = 35

w = fx*dx

# solve using work-energy theorem
v2 = sym.Symbol('v2')
eq = w - (0.5*m*v2**2 - 0.5*m*v1**2)
sol = sym.solve(eq,v2)
v2 = sol[1]
print('The speed of the pumpkin right before impact is '+str(v2)+' m/s.')

Detailed Solutions#

Problem D5.1

Problem D5.1

Problem
A 5.0 kg box is being pushed across a horizontal surface through a +235 N net force. The box is initially at rest. After being pushed a distance of 45 m,

  1. how much work was done on the box?

  2. what is the kinetic energy at 45 m?

  3. how fast is it traveling at 45 m?

  4. what is its velocity at 45 m?

Methodology
To find the work, we simply plug and chuck numbers into the definition of work. Since work is the change in kinetic energy and the box is initially at rest, the final kinetic energy must be equal the amount of work put into it. We can then use the definition of kinetic energy to find the speed. Since the force is acting in the positive direction, and the box initially was at rest, we conclude that the box must be moving in the positive direction. Hence, we can assign a direction to the speed a.k.a velocity.

Solution
Part 1:
The work is

\( w = f_{net,x}\Delta x = (+235)(+45) = 10,575 \rightarrow 1.1\times 10^4~\textrm{J} \)

Part 2:
Through the work-energy theorem we have

\( \Delta k = w \)

Since the particle is initially at rest (\(k_i = 0\)), the final kinetic energy must be the work put into the box:

\(k_f = 1.1\times 10^4~\textrm{J} \)

Part 3:
Since we know the kinetic energy, we can solve for the speed (use the non-rounded answer for work):

\( \frac{1}{2}mv_f^2 = 10,575 \)

\( v_f = \sqrt{ \frac{(2)(10,575)}{(5.0)} } = 65~\textrm{m/s} \)

Part 4:
The velocity is

\(v_x = +65~\textrm{m/s} \)

Problem D5.2

Problem D5.2

Problem

During a pumpkin drop, a 9.5 kg pumpkin is dropped from the roof of a 35 m building. The force exterted by gravity on the pumpkin is \( f_g = mg \), where \(g = 9.8~\textrm{m/s}^2\). Ignore air resistance. How fast is the pumpkin traveling upon impact with the ground assuming it is being thrown downwards with a speed of 25 m/s?

Methodology
Since we ignore friction, the force of gravity must be the net force acting on the pumpkin. We can then use the work-energy theorem to solve for the speed before impact.

Solution
The work-energy theorem states that

\( \Delta k = w \)

If we write out the kinetic energies and work, we have

\( \frac{1}{2}mv_f^2 - \frac{1}{2}mv_i^2 = f_{net,z}\Delta z \)

where I am choosing \(z\) to be the vertical direction positive downwards. Solving for the final speed and inserting numbers (and using \(f_{net,z} = mg\)):

\( v_f = \sqrt{ v_{i}^2 + \frac{2mg\Delta x}{m} } = \sqrt{ (+25)^2 + \frac{(2)(9.5)(+9.8)(+35)}{(9.5)} } = 36~\textrm{m/s} \)
