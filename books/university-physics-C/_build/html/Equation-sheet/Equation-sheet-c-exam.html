Equation Sheet for C Exam#

These are the basic concepts and equations you will need for the C exam, not including mathematical tools such as trigonometry and dot product.

Restrictions/Assumptions#

  • We are only considering point mass/particle objects.

  • We are only using differences (quantities with \(\Delta\) in front), and quantities calculated from differences are averages. For example: \(\vec{v} = \frac{\Delta \vec{r}}{\Delta t}\) is the average velocity during the time interval \(\Delta t\).

Concept

Equation

Component Example

Comments

Point mass/particle

An object where size and shape does not play a role in the dynamics of the object.

Inertial Mass

\(m\)

Resistance to a change in linear motion.

Position

\(\vec{r}\)

\(x\)

Location of an object specified with respect to a given reference frame.

Linear Displacement

\(\Delta\vec{r}\)

\(\Delta x\)

Change in position between events 1 and 2. Can be either positive or negative.

Directed line segment pointing from location of event 1 to location of event 2.

Linear Path Length

\(\Delta s\)

Distance traveled between events 1 and 2. Not a vector and always positive.

Elapsed Time

\(\Delta t\)

Difference in time between events 1 and 2.

Average Linear Velocity

\(\vec{v} = \frac{\Delta \vec{r}}{\Delta t}\)

\(v_x = \frac{\Delta x}{\Delta t}\)

Linear displacement over an elapsed time interval. How fast an object is moving in a certain direction. Can be positive or negative.

Average Linear Speed

\(v = \frac{\Delta s}{\Delta t}\)

Linear path length between events 1 and 2 over an elapsed time interval. How fast an object is moving. Always positive.

Average Linear Acceleration

\(\vec{a} = \frac{\Delta \vec{v}}{\Delta t}\)

\(a_x = \frac{\Delta v_{x}}{\Delta t}\)

Change in linear velocity over an elapsed time interval. Can be positive or negative.

1st Derived Equation of Motion

\(\vec{r} = \vec{r}_{0} + \vec{v}_{0}t + \frac{1}{2}\vec{a}t^2 \)

\(x = x_0 + v_{0x}t + \frac{1}{2}a_{x}t^2\)

Change in linear velocity over an elapsed time interval. Can be positive or negative.

2nd Derived Equation of Motion

\(v_{x}^2 = v_{0x}^2 + 2a_{x}\Delta x\)

Torrecilli’s Equation. Only valid for constant acceleration and not a vector equation.

Linear Momentum

\(\vec{p} = m\vec{v}\)

\(p_x = mv_x\)

Definition of linear motion in x-direcion (or y or z). Can be positive or negative.

Change in Linear Momentum

\(\Delta \vec{p} = \vec{p}_{2} - \vec{p}_{1}\)

\(\Delta p_x = p_{2_{x}} - p_{1_{x}}\)

Change in linear motion between events 1 and 2.

\(\Delta \vec{p} = m\Delta \vec{v}\)

\(\Delta p_x = m\Delta v_x\)

Only true for a point particle or object that does not change mass between events 1 and 2.

Impulse

\(\vec{j} = \vec{f}\Delta t\)

\( j_x = f_{x}\Delta t\)

Impulse delivered by a single force.

Impulse-Momentum Theorem

\(\vec{j}_{net} = \Delta\vec{p}\)

\( j_{net,x} = \Delta p_x\)

Impulse delivered by net force is the change in momentum of the particle.

\( \vec{f}_{net}\Delta t = m\vec{v}_2 + m\vec{v}_1\)

\( f_{net,x}\Delta t = mv_{2x} - mv_{1x} \)

Cause and Effect: Cause (force) acting on an object through some time interval will change the motion (transfer momentum) of the object between events 1 and 2.

Average Linear Net Force

\(\vec{f}_{net} = \frac{\Delta \vec{p}}{\Delta t}\)

\(f_{{net}_{x}} = \frac{\Delta p_{x}}{\Delta t}\)

Cause and Effect: Cause (force) of change in motion (effect) between events 1 and 2.

Also known as Newton’s 2nd Law.

\(\vec{f}_{net} = m\vec{a}\)

\(f_{net_{x}} = ma_x\)

Only true for a point particle or object that does not change mass between events 1 and 2.

Probably the one most people associate with Newton’s 2nd Law from everyday life.

Kinetic Energy

\(k = \frac{1}{2}m\vec{v}\cdot\vec{v}\)

\(k = \frac{1}{2}m\sqrt{v_{x}^2 + v_{y}^2}\)

Measure of energy related to motion. Not a vector.

Mechanical Work

\(w = \vec{f}\cdot\Delta\vec{r}\)

\(w = f_{x}\Delta x + f_{y}\Delta y \)

Transfer of particle/system energy between events 1 and 2 due to force \(\vec{f}\). Work is not energy, but transfer of energy.

Positive: Energy input to particle. Negative: Energy out from particle.

Work-Energy Theorem

\(w_{net} = \Delta k\)

Net energy transfer is changing the kinetic energy of a particle.

\(\vec{f}_{net}\cdot\Delta\vec{r} = \frac{1}{2}mv_{2}^2 - \frac{1}{2}mv_{1}^2\)

Example working equation of work-energy theorem.

Conservative Force

\(w_{closed~path} = 0\)

Energy transfer along a closed path is zero if the force is conservative.

Potential Energy

\(\Delta u = -w_C\)

Only defined for conservative forces.

Gravitational Potential Energy

\(u_g = mgh\)

\(h\) is some height above a reference point.

Mechanical Energy

\(e = k + u\)

Total mechanical energy.

Work-Energy Theorem Again

\(w_{NC} = e_2 - e_1\)

Only non-conservative forces can change the mechanical energy of a particle.

Working Equation for Work-Energy

\(\frac{1}{2}mv_{1}^2 + mgh_1 + w_{NC} = \frac{1}{2}mv_{2}^2 + mgh_2\)
